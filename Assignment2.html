ECONOMIC ANALYSIS

Economic indicators are statistics that provide a more precise understanding of the current economic landscape. They include key metrics such as Gross Domestic Product (GDP), the Unemployment Rate, and Trade . These indicators play a pivotal role in guiding financial decisions and long-term economic planning.

With the help of these forecasts, both consumers and businesses can make informed choices regarding investments, workforce, and financial strategies.

Key Economic Metrics

The Gross Domestic Product (GDP) is a widely-used statistic for assessing a nation's economic health. It quantifies the total value of goods and services produced over time. In 2020, the COVID-19 pandemic had a profound impact on economies worldwide.

Unemployment reflects the health of an economy and its labor utilization. High unemployment rates have adverse effects on individuals and the economy.

Annual GDP v Trade Contribution

Trade , measured as the share of GDP, signifies a country's engagement in international trade. It gauges the extent to which a nation participates in global trade, reflecting its economic orientation. This openness plays a pivotal role in stimulating economic growth, job generation, and poverty alleviation. Trade enhances productivity, creates local business prospects, and encourages innovation through market competition, all of which affect GDP and other economic indicators.

The bubble plot demonstrates that trade openness has varying effects. It's negative for low-development nations like Burundi but less impactful in highly developed countries like Hong Kong, Luxembourg and other countries.

Countries with High Unemployment

Nations such as South Africa, Macedonia and Palestine deal with elevated levels of unemployment. This situation arises from various factors, including insufficient access to education and training, limited capital resources and pervasive poverty.
